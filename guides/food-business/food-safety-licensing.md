# 🧼 Food Safety & Licensing Guide for Bela Bela

## Operating a Legal and Safe Food Business

**Essential guide for food hygiene, safety, and legal requirements in Bela Bela**

## 🏛️ Legal Requirements in South Africa

### Municipal Registration:

**Certificate of Acceptability (COA):**
- **Required for:** All food businesses
- **Issued by:** Modimolle-Mookgophong Local Municipality
- **Cost:** R150-500 (annual renewal)
- **Process:** Apply at Municipal Offices
- **Documents needed:**
  - ID copy
  - Proof of address
  - Site plan (if premises-based)
  - Food safety plan
  - Water quality certificate (if not municipal water)

**Where to Apply:**
- Modimolle-Mookgophong Municipality
- Health Department
- Address: Modimolle Municipal Offices
- Phone: Check current number at municipal offices

**Inspection:**
- Health inspector will visit
- Check premises, equipment, water supply
- Review your food handling practices
- May require improvements before approval
- Re-inspection annually

### Home-Based Food Business:

**What You Can Do:**
- Prepare food at home for sale
- Must meet safety standards
- Kitchen must be clean and separate from family use (ideally)
- Same hygiene rules apply

**Limitations:**
- Usually for small-scale operations
- May restrict certain high-risk foods
- Check with municipality

### Street Vending:

**Trading Permit Required:**
- Cost: R50-200 per year
- Designated areas only
- Health certificate required
- Can be mobile or stationary

**Trading Areas in Bela Bela:**
- Market square (Saturday market)
- Designated street vendor zones
- Near taxi rank (with permit)
- Special events (temporary permits)

## 🧼 Food Safety Basics (Essential!)

### Personal Hygiene:

**Before Starting Work:**
1. **Wash hands thoroughly:**
   - Warm water and soap
   - Scrub for 20 seconds
   - Between fingers, under nails
   - Dry with clean towel or air dry

**When to Wash Hands:**
✅ Before preparing food
✅ After using toilet
✅ After touching raw meat/eggs
✅ After handling money
✅ After touching face/hair
✅ After smoking
✅ After sneezing/coughing
✅ After handling garbage

**Personal Cleanliness:**
- **Clean clothes** - Change daily, use apron
- **Hair tied back** - Cover with cap/scarf
- **Short nails** - Clean, no nail polish
- **No jewelry** - Can harbor bacteria
- **Wounds covered** - Waterproof bandage + gloves

**When You Cannot Work:**
❌ Diarrhea or vomiting
❌ Skin infection (especially on hands)
❌ Eye infection
❌ Fever or flu
❌ Open wounds (unless properly covered)

---

### Kitchen/Food Preparation Area:

**Requirements:**

**Surfaces:**
- Clean, smooth, washable
- No cracks or chips
- Stainless steel or plastic (best)
- Sanitize before and after use

**Cleaning Schedule:**
- **During work:** Wipe spills immediately
- **After each prep session:** Full clean with soap
- **Daily:** Deep clean all surfaces
- **Weekly:** Scrub walls, floors, equipment

**Cleaning Method:**
1. Remove food debris
2. Wash with hot soapy water
3. Rinse with clean water
4. Sanitize (bleach solution: 1 cap per 5L water)
5. Air dry or dry with clean cloth

**Floors:**
- Sweep after each session
- Mop daily with disinfectant
- No standing water
- Good drainage

**Waste:**
- Covered bin with lid
- Empty at least daily
- Keep away from food prep area
- Wash bin weekly
- Burn or bury far from kitchen

---

### Food Storage (Critical for Bela Bela Heat):

**Temperature Control:**

**Cold Storage (Below 5°C):**
- Refrigerator essential for high-risk foods
- Check temperature daily
- Raw meat: Lowest shelf (prevent dripping)
- Cooked food: Top shelves (protected)
- **No fridge?** Use daily shopping, sell quickly

**Hot Storage (Above 60°C):**
- Keep hot food hot
- Use insulated containers
- Reheat thoroughly if cooling
- Don't leave food at room temperature over 2 hours

**Dry Storage:**
- Cool, dry place
- Off the ground (pallets, shelves)
- Away from chemicals
- First In, First Out (FIFO - use older stock first)
- Cover to protect from pests

**Bela Bela Heat Warning:**
- Food spoils FASTER in hot weather (30-40°C)
- **Danger zone:** 5-60°C (bacteria multiply rapidly)
- Cook and sell same day whenever possible
- Don't leave food in car/sun

### Water Safety:

**Municipal Water:**
- Generally safe for Bela Bela
- If any doubt, boil for cooking

**Borehole/Well Water:**
- **Must test** before using for food
- Test at lab (Polokwane)
- Cost: R200-500
- Boil if any doubt

**Ice:**
- Only from safe water source
- Store in clean containers
- Handle with tongs/scoop (never hands)

## 🍖 Handling Different Foods

### Raw Meat/Chicken:

**High Risk - Extra Care:**

**Buying:**
- Fresh smell (not sour)
- Good color
- Not slimy
- Cold when buying

**Storage:**
- Refrigerate immediately
- Use within 2 days
- Or freeze immediately
- Separate from other foods

**Preparation:**
- Separate cutting board (NEVER use same for vegetables)
- Separate knife
- Wash hands after handling
- Clean all surfaces touched
- Cook thoroughly (no pink inside)

**Cooking Temperatures:**
- Chicken: 75°C internal (or juices run clear)
- Beef: 70°C (well done)
- Pork: 70°C

**Never:**
❌ Reuse marinade
❌ Taste raw meat
❌ Leave at room temperature
❌ Partially cook then finish later

---

### Vegetables and Fruits:

**Washing Essential:**

**Process:**
1. Rinse under running water
2. Rub surface (remove dirt)
3. Soak in clean water 5 minutes
4. Rinse again
5. Dry with clean cloth or air dry

**For Salads:**
- Wash extra thoroughly
- Pat dry
- Keep cold until serving
- Make fresh daily (don't store cut salad)

**Cutting:**
- Use clean cutting board
- Never same one as raw meat
- Wash fruits/vegetables BEFORE cutting

---

### Cooked Food:

**Cooling:**
- Cool quickly if not serving immediately
- Shallow containers (cools faster)
- Don't cover while hot (traps heat)
- Refrigerate within 2 hours

**Reheating:**
- Heat thoroughly (75°C or steaming hot throughout)
- Only reheat once
- Stir while heating (even temperature)
- Don't reheat portions (all or nothing)

**Holding Hot Food:**
- Keep above 60°C
- Use insulated containers
- Don't keep over 4 hours
- Check temperature regularly

---

### Oils and Fats:

**Frying Safety:**
- Never leave hot oil unattended
- Don't overfill pot
- Use long utensils
- Keep water away (splatters)
- Fire extinguisher or sand nearby

**Oil Reuse:**
- Strain after each use
- Check for burnt smell
- Discard if dark or smoking
- Maximum 3-4 uses
- Store in clean, covered container

## 🐀 Pest Control:

### Prevention (Cheapest Method):

**Rodents:**
✅ No food left out overnight
✅ All food in sealed containers
✅ Garbage removed daily
✅ No water sources (fix drips)
✅ Block entry holes
✅ Keep area clean

**Flies:**
✅ Cover all food
✅ Screen windows/doors
✅ Clean spills immediately
✅ Remove garbage regularly
✅ Don't leave dirty dishes

**Cockroaches:**
✅ No food debris
✅ No standing water
✅ Seal cracks
✅ Clean behind equipment
✅ Proper garbage disposal

### Control Methods:

**Traps:**
- Rat/mouse traps: R15-50 each
- Check and empty daily
- Place away from food areas

**Natural Repellents:**
- Peppermint oil (rats)
- Bay leaves (cockroaches)
- Citrus peels (ants)

**Professional:**
- Pest control service: R300-800 per visit
- Consider if serious infestation
- Quarterly service for prevention

## 📋 Food Safety Plan (Required for COA):

### Simple Template:

**1. Identify Hazards:**
List potential problems:
- Raw chicken (bacterial contamination)
- Food left in heat (spoilage)
- Dirty water (contamination)
- Unclean hands (cross-contamination)

**2. Control Measures:**
What you do to prevent:
- Cook chicken to 75°C
- Refrigerate or sell within 2 hours
- Use safe water source
- Wash hands before preparation

**3. Monitoring:**
How you check it's working:
- Check food temperature with thermometer
- Check expiry dates daily
- Visual inspection of cleanliness
- Regular hand washing reminders

**4. Corrective Actions:**
What you do if problem:
- Discard food if temperature wrong
- Stop using suspect ingredients
- Extra cleaning if pests seen
- Retrain on hand washing if needed

**5. Records:**
Keep track of:
- Daily temperature checks
- Cleaning schedules
- Supplier invoices (traceability)
- Staff health/training

## 🚨 Food Poisoning Prevention:

### Common Causes in Bela Bela:

**1. Heat (Most Common):**
- Food left in hot weather
- Inadequate refrigeration
- Long time between cooking and eating
- **Prevention:** Cook and sell quickly, keep cold/hot

**2. Poor Hand Hygiene:**
- Not washing hands properly
- Touching food after money/toilet
- **Prevention:** Strict hand washing routine

**3. Cross-Contamination:**
- Raw meat touching cooked food
- Same utensils for raw and cooked
- **Prevention:** Separate equipment, good cleaning

**4. Undercooked Food:**
- Chicken/meat not fully cooked
- **Prevention:** Proper cooking, check temperature

### If Customer Gets Sick:

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Stop selling immediately
2. Keep samples of ingredients
3. Review your process
4. Report to health department
5. Get medical advice
6. Fix the problem before restarting

**Prevention Better Than Cure:**
- Follow all safety procedures
- Don't take shortcuts
- When in doubt, throw out
- Your reputation depends on safety
