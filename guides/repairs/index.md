# 🔧 Repairs Guide

## Make Money Fixing Things

**People need things fixed - cheaper than buying new!**

---

## 🎯 Types of Repair Services

### 1. Bicycle Repairs 🚲
**Very popular, low investment**

**Common Repairs:**
- Flat tire/puncture: $0.50-1.00
- Brake adjustment: $1.00-2.00
- Chain repair: $1.00-3.00
- Gear adjustment: $1.00-2.00
- Full service: $5.00-10.00

**What You Need:**
- **Tools:** Tire levers, patches, glue, pump, wrenches, screwdrivers
- **Supplies:** Patches, tubes, oil, grease
- **Money to start:** $30-60

**How to Start:**
1. Learn from bike shop (offer to help free)
2. Practice on your own bike
3. Start with puncture repairs only
4. Expand as you learn
5. Set up at busy location

**Daily Income:**
- 15-30 repairs
- **Profit:** $15-40 per day

**Tips:**
✅ Fast service = more customers
✅ Keep busy location (market, school, bus stop)
✅ Clean hands after each job
✅ Explain what you fixed

### 2. Shoe Repairs 👞
**Always needed, simple to learn**

**Services:**
- Sole replacement: $2-5
- Heel repair: $2-4
- Stitching repair: $1-3
- Polish service: $0.50-1.00
- Stretching: $1-2

**What You Need:**
- **Tools:** Knife, hammer, awl, needle, brush
- **Supplies:** Glue, thread, soles, heels, polish
- **Money to start:** $40-80

**How to Start:**
1. Learn from experienced cobbler
2. Buy basic supplies
3. Start with simple repairs (stitching)
4. Add sole replacement as you learn
5. Location near market or bus stop

**Daily Income:**
- 10-20 repairs
- **Profit:** $15-35 per day

**Tips:**
✅ Keep shoes clean while working
✅ Fast turnaround (same day)
✅ Store uncollected shoes safely
✅ Write receipt with date

---

### 3. Electronics/Phone Repairs 📱
**Higher profit, needs training**

**Services:**
- Screen replacement: $10-30
- Battery replacement: $5-15
- Charging port repair: $5-10
- Software/reset: $2-5
- Button repair: $3-8

**What You Need:**
- **Tools:** Screwdriver set, tweezers, heat gun/hair dryer
- **Parts:** Common screens, batteries, ports
- **Knowledge:** Learn from courses or experienced person
- **Money to start:** $100-200

**How to Start:**
1. **Learn first** - Very important!
2. Get training or apprentice
3. Practice on old/broken phones
4. Start with simple repairs
5. Build part suppliers relationships

**Daily Income:**
- 3-8 repairs
- **Profit:** $20-60 per day

**Warning:**
⚠️ Need real training - can damage expensive phones
⚠️ Need parts suppliers
⚠️ Competition from phone shops
⚠️ Keep customer data private

---

### 4. Small Appliance Repairs 🔌
**Fans, radios, irons, small electrics**

**Common Repairs:**
- Fans not working: $3-8
- Iron not heating: $3-6
- Radio no sound: $3-8
- Blender broken: $5-10
- Extension cord repair: $1-3

**What You Need:**
- **Tools:** Screwdrivers, wire cutters, multimeter, soldering iron
- **Parts:** Wires, plugs, fuses, switches
- **Knowledge:** Basic electrical
- **Money to start:** $50-100

**How to Start:**
1. Learn electrical basics (safety!)
2. Start with simple repairs (cords, plugs)
3. Open broken appliances to learn
4. Advance to circuit repairs
5. Never work on items plugged in!

**Daily Income:**
- 5-10 repairs
- **Profit:** $15-40 per day

**Safety:**
⚠️ **UNPLUG** before working
⚠️ Don't touch live wires
⚠️ Test carefully
⚠️ If unsure, don't risk it

## 📈 Starting Your Repair Business

### Week 1: Choose and Learn
- Pick 1 repair type to start
- Learn from expert (offer free help)
- Practice on old items
- Buy basic tools only

### Week 2-4: Start Small
- Offer cheap prices initially
- Work from home or busy location
- Tell everyone your service
- Build reputation with quality

### Month 2-3: Grow
- Add more repair types
- Invest in better tools
- Raise prices to fair level
- Keep regular location/hours

### Month 4+: Establish
- Known for quality
- Regular customers
- Full range of services
- Perhaps hire assistant

## 🎯 Finding Customers

### Best Locations:
1. **Market entrance** - High foot traffic
2. **Bus/taxi station** - Waiting customers
3. **Near schools** - Bicycles, bags
4. **Busy street corner** - Visible
5. **Outside phone/electronic shop** - Repairs while they shop

### Marketing:
- **Signs** - Clear, visible
- **Word of mouth** - Best advertising
- **Quality work** - Reputation spreads
- **Fair prices** - Not cheapest, not expensive
- **Fast service** - When possible

### Building Reputation:
✅ Fix it right first time
✅ Explain what was wrong
✅ Give receipt/ticket
✅ Keep promises (time/price)
✅ Be honest if you can't fix it

---

## 💰 Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Rules:
1. **Know parts cost** - Buy cheap, don't overcharge
2. **Value your time** - Skill worth money
3. **Match market** - Know competitor prices
4. **Complex = More** - Difficult jobs cost more
5. **Build in profit** - At least 50-100%

### Example (Bicycle Puncture):
- Patch: $0.10
- Glue: $0.05
- Your time: 10 minutes
- **Charge:** $0.50-1.00
- **Profit:** $0.35-0.85

### When to Refuse:
- Too damaged to fix safely
- Repair cost more than replacement
- Don't have skill/parts
- Customer wants unrealistic price

**Better to refuse than do bad job!**

## ⚠️ Common Problems and Solutions

### Problem: Not enough customers
**Solution:**
- Better location (move if needed)
- Lower prices temporarily
- Offer quick service
- Advertise more
- Add new services

### Problem: Can't fix item
**Solution:**
- Be honest immediately
- Don't charge if you tried
- Refer to someone who can
- Learn from it for next time

### Problem: Customer disputes price
**Solution:**
- Quote price BEFORE starting
- Explain what you did
- Show replaced parts
- Stand firm but polite
- Learn to estimate better

### Problem: Not enough tools/parts
**Solution:**
- Start with most common repairs
- Buy tools gradually
- Share/borrow specialized tools
- Build parts inventory slowly
- Know where to get parts quickly

## 📝 Daily Work Routine

**Morning (7am-12pm):**
- Set up workspace
- Work on overnight jobs
- Handle walk-in repairs
- Quick jobs while customer waits

**Afternoon (1pm-6pm):**
- Continue repairs
- Restock parts if needed
- Longer/complex repairs
- Clean and organize

**Evening:**
- Close up shop
- Count money
- List parts needed
- Plan tomorrow

**Weekly:**
- Buy parts/supplies
- Tool maintenance
- Deep clean workspace
- Marketing activities

---

## 🛠️ Tool Maintenance

**Daily:**
- Clean tools after use
- Check for damage
- Put tools away properly

**Weekly:**
- Oil moving parts
- Sharpen blades if needed
- Tighten loose handles

**Monthly:**
- Check all tools work properly
- Replace worn items
- Organize tool storage

**Good tools = Good work = Happy customers!**

## 🌟 Success Examples

**Example 1: Bicycle Repair**
- Started with $40 (basic tools)
- 20 repairs/day x $0.75 profit = $15/day
- After 3 months: Better location, tools, $25-35/day
- After 6 months: Helper, expanded services, $40-60/day

**Example 2: Shoe Repair**
- Started near market, $50 investment
- 15 repairs/day x $1.50 profit = $22/day
- Built reputation for quality
- Regular customers, $25-40/day consistently

**Example 3: Phone Repair**
- Took 2-month training course
- Started with screen replacements only
- $150 investment in tools/parts
- 5 repairs/day x $8 profit = $40/day
- Expanded to batteries, software, more
