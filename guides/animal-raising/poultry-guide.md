## 🏠 Housing for Bela Bela Climate

### Basic Requirements:

**Size:**
- Layers: 4 birds per m² (10 birds = 2.5m²)
- Broilers: 10 birds per m² (50 birds = 5m²)
- **Better:** Give more space (healthier birds)

**Protection Needed:**
- **Predators:** Dogs, cats, snakes, mongooses, birds of prey
- **Weather:** Hot sun (30-40°C), occasional rain, cold nights (5-10°C winter)
- **Theft:** Secure locking (unfortunately necessary)

### Building a Coop:

**Materials (for 10 layers):**
- Poles/wood: R150-300
- Wire mesh: R200-400
- Roofing (corrugated iron): R150-300
- Nesting boxes materials: R50-100
- **Total: R550-1100**

**Or:**
- Convert existing structure: R100-300
- Use recycled materials: R50-200

**Design Features:**

**Ventilation (Critical in Bela Bela):**
- Windows/openings on opposite sides
- Covered with mesh (predator protection)
- Allow cross-breeze (hot summers)
- Can close partially in winter

**Roof:**
- Insulated or double layer (heat protection)
- Overhang 50cm (shade and rain protection)
- Face north if possible (less direct sun)

**Floor:**
- Raised off ground (rats, moisture)
- Solid (easier to clean)
- Slight slope for drainage
- Covered with bedding (wood shavings, sawdust)

**Nesting Boxes (Layers):**
- 1 box per 4 hens
- Size: 30cm x 30cm x 30cm
- Dark and private
- Soft bedding inside
- Easy access for egg collection

**Perches:**
- 20cm per bird
- 40-50cm high
- Smooth wood (splinters = foot problems)
- Easy to remove for cleaning

**Run/Outdoor Area:**
- Enclosed with mesh
- Shade available (trees, shade cloth)
- Dust bath area (chickens love this)
- Size: 2-4m² per 10 birds

### Heat Management (Bela Bela Specific):

**Hot Season (Oct-March):**
✅ Plenty of shade
✅ Good ventilation
✅ Fresh water always (change twice daily)
✅ Feed early morning (cooler)
✅ Wet sand in run (cooling)
✅ Avoid overcrowding

**Signs of Heat Stress:**
- Panting heavily
- Wings held away from body
- Lying down more
- Reduced egg production
- Drinking lots of water

**Action:** Increase ventilation, more shade, cool water

## 🐣 Starting with Layers

### Buying Point-of-Lay Hens:

**Best Choice for Beginners:**
- Age: 18-20 weeks (about to start laying)
- Cost: R80-120 per hen
- Advantage: Start earning immediately
- Where: Hatcheries in Polokwane, Modimolle

**What to Look For:**
- Bright red comb (healthy)
- Clear, bright eyes
- Active and alert
- Clean feathers
- No discharge from nose/eyes

**Breeds for Bela Bela:**
- **Hyline Brown:** Best layers, heat-tolerant
- **Lohmann Brown:** Good layers, hardy
- **Black Australorp:** Dual purpose, calm
- **Avoid:** Light breeds (less heat-tolerant)

### First Week Care:

1. **Arrival:**
   - Put in coop immediately
   - Show water first
   - Add electrolytes to water (R15 packet)
   - Offer food after 2 hours
   - Keep confined 3 days (learn home)

2. **Daily Care:**
   - Check water 2-3 times (hot weather)
   - Feed morning and evening
   - Observe behavior
   - Collect eggs daily

3. **Watch For:**
   - Stress (relocation stress normal 2-3 days)
   - Bullying (separate aggressive birds)
   - Disease signs
   - Start of laying (usually 1-2 weeks)

### Feeding Layers:

**Types of Feed:**
- **Layers pellets:** R240-320 per 50kg
- **Layers mash:** R220-300 per 50kg
- **Crumbles:** R260-340 per 50kg

**Amount:**
- 120-150g per bird per day
- 10 birds = 1.2-1.5kg daily
- 50kg bag = 30-40 days for 10 birds

**Feeding Schedule:**
- Morning: 1/3 of daily amount
- Evening: 2/3 of daily amount
- Always available water

**Supplements:**
- **Grit:** R15-25 per 2kg (aids digestion)
- **Oyster shell:** R20-30 per 2kg (strong eggshells)
- **Kitchen scraps:** Supplement (not main diet)

**DO Feed:**
✅ Vegetable scraps
✅ Fruit (not too much)
✅ Cooked rice/porridge
✅ Bread (small amounts)
✅ Greens (grass, weeds)

**DON'T Feed:**
❌ Raw potato peels
❌ Avocado
❌ Chocolate
❌ Salty foods
❌ Moldy anything

### Egg Production:

**Expect:**
- Start: 18-22 weeks old
- Peak: 24-32 weeks (almost daily eggs)
- Good production: 1 year
- Decline: After 18 months
- Each hen: 250-300 eggs per year

**Bela Bela Seasonal Variations:**
- **Summer:** Slightly lower production (heat stress)
- **Winter:** Good production (cool weather)
- **Molting season:** Stop or reduce (annual feather replacement)

**Egg Collection:**
- Collect 2-3 times daily (hot weather)
- Morning collection most important
- Check for cracked/dirty eggs
- Store in cool place
- Don't wash until selling (removes protective coating)

### Common Layer Problems:

**Problem: Not Laying**
- **Age:** Too young or too old
- **Stress:** Recent move, predator scare
- **Season:** Molting period
- **Health:** Check for signs of illness
- **Solution:** Time, reduce stress, check health

**Problem: Soft-shelled Eggs**
- **Cause:** Calcium deficiency
- **Solution:** Provide oyster shell, quality feed

**Problem: Egg Eating**
- **Cause:** Boredom, protein deficiency, accident discovered
- **Solution:** Collect frequently, provide enough protein, fake eggs in nests

## 🍗 Starting with Broilers

### Buying Day-Old Chicks:

**Source:**
- Hatcheries: Polokwane, Pretoria
- Cost: R10-18 per chick
- Minimum order: Usually 50 chicks
- Best: Collect yourself (less stress)

**Breeds:**
- **Ross 308:** Fast growing, 6-7 weeks
- **Cobb 500:** Good feed conversion
- **Both:** Industry standard, proven

### Brooder Setup (First 3-4 Weeks):

**Critical Period - High Attention Needed**

**Heat Source:**
- **Essential:** Chicks need 32-35°C first week
- **Options:**
  - Heat lamp (250W bulb): R150-250
  - Gas brooder: R800-1500
  - Charcoal brazier: R50-150 (careful monitoring)
- **Schedule:**
  - Week 1: 32-35°C
  - Week 2: 29-32°C
  - Week 3: 26-29°C
  - Week 4: 23-26°C (then normal)

**Brooder Space:**
- First week: 50 chicks = 2m²
- Expand weekly as they grow
- By week 4: 50 chicks = 5m²

**Brooder Setup:**
1. Clean and disinfect area
2. Cover floor with newspaper first day
3. Add wood shavings after day 2
4. Set up heat source (test temperature first)
5. Place water near edge (not under heat)
6. Place feed trays
7. Add chicks gently

**First 48 Hours (Most Critical):**
- Check temperature constantly
- Dip beaks in water (teach to drink)
- Show food after 2 hours
- Add electrolytes to water
- Watch for piling (sign of cold - adjust heat)
- Watch for spreading (sign of hot - reduce heat)

### Feeding Broilers:

**Types of Feed:**
- **Starter (0-3 weeks):** R350-450 per 50kg
- **Grower (3-6 weeks):** R320-420 per 50kg
- **Finisher (6-8 weeks):** R300-400 per 50kg

**Feed Consumption (50 birds):**
- Week 1-3: 75kg starter
- Week 3-6: 150kg grower
- Week 6-8: 100kg finisher
- **Total: 325kg for 50 birds to market**

**Feeding Schedule:**
- Always available (free choice)
- Fresh feed daily
- Clean feeders every 3 days
- Plenty of feeder space (no crowding)

**Water:**
- Critical - more important than feed
- Fresh water 24/7
- Change 2-3 times daily (hot weather)
- Clean waterers daily

### Growth and Market:

**Target:**
- Age: 6-8 weeks
- Weight: 2.0-2.5kg live weight
- Ready when: Plump breast, legs strong

**Market Options:**

**Live Sales:**
- Price: R55-75 per bird (2kg)
- Buyers: Individuals, butchers
- Advantage: No slaughter needed
- Challenge: Find buyers ahead

**Dressed (Slaughtered):**
- Price: R65-95 per bird
- Buyers: Direct customers, shops
- Requires: Slaughter setup, health permit
- Higher profit but more work

### Broiler Health Management:

**Vaccination Schedule:**
- Day 1: Marek's (usually done at hatchery)
- Day 7: Newcastle Disease (in drinking water)
- Day 14: Infectious Bursal Disease (Gumboro)
- Day 21: Newcastle booster

**Cost:** R50-100 for 50 birds
**Where:** Agricultural suppliers, vets

**Common Problems:**

**High Mortality (First Week):**
- Usually temperature or water issues
- Check brooder setup immediately
- Consult supplier or vet

**Leg Problems:**
- Growing too fast
- Reduce feed slightly
- Ensure good ventilation

**Coccidiosis (Most Common):**
- Signs: Bloody droppings, listless
- Prevention: Medicated feed, clean conditions
- Treatment: Coccidiostat in water (R80-150)

## 🏥 Health and Biosecurity

### Daily Health Checks:

**Look For:**
- Active, alert birds
- Normal eating and drinking
- Clean eyes and nostrils
- Normal droppings
- No coughing or sneezing
- Good feather condition

**Sick Bird Signs:**
- Standing alone, hunched
- Not eating/drinking
- Discharge from eyes/nose
- Abnormal droppings
- Coughing, wheezing
- Swollen head or legs

**Action:**
1. Isolate sick bird immediately
2. Provide electrolytes
3. Consult vet or agricultural extension
4. Don't delay treatment

### Biosecurity (Disease Prevention):

✅ Limit visitors to coop
✅ Footbath at entrance (dilute bleach)
✅ Change clothes before entering
✅ Don't visit other chicken farms then yours same day
✅ Quarantine new birds 2 weeks
✅ Clean and disinfect regularly
✅ Control rodents and wild birds
✅ Proper disposal of dead birds (bury or burn)

### Vaccination Important:

- Protects flock
- Cheap compared to losing birds
- Required for some markets
- Contact: Agricultural extension officer for schedule

## 📅 Bela Bela Seasonal Considerations

### Hot Season (Oct-March):

**Challenges:**
- Heat stress
- More water needed
- Slower growth (broilers)
- Slightly lower egg production

**Solutions:**
- Extra ventilation
- Shade structures
- Wet sand in run
- Feed in cool hours

### Cool Season (April-Sept):

**Advantages:**
- Better growth rates
- Higher egg production
- Less disease pressure
- Easier management

**Considerations:**
- Protect from cold nights (close coops)
- Still provide ventilation
- Watch for respiratory issues
