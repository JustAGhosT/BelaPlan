- 3-5 repairs

## 💰 Pricing Your Work

### Calculate Costs:
1. **Materials** - Wood, hardware, finish
2. **Transport** - Getting materials
3. **Tool wear** - Blades, sandpaper, etc.
4. **Your time** - Value your skill!
5. **Profit** - At least 30-50%

### Pricing Methods:

**By Project:**
- Calculate all costs
- Add labor (fair wage for hours)
- Add 30-50% profit

**By Day:**
- Charge daily rate
- Customer buys materials
- Good for repairs/renovations

**Example (Simple Bench):**
- Wood: $12
- Screws/nails: $2
- Sandpaper: $1
- Your time: 4 hours x $3 = $12
- **Cost:** $27
- **Sell for:** $35-40
- **Profit:** $8-13

## ⚠️ Common Problems and Solutions

### Problem: Not enough customers
**Solution:**
- Make samples (display outside home)
- Offer first project discount
- Visit schools/offices with photos
- Partner with builders
- Quality brings referrals

### Problem: Materials too expensive
**Solution:**
- Buy in bulk with other carpenters
- Use reclaimed wood (old furniture)
- Shop around for best prices
- Start with cheaper wood types
- Customer supplies material (you charge labor)

### Problem: Mistake in cutting
**Solution:**
- Measure twice, cut once (always!)
- Practice on scrap first
- Use sharp tools (easier to control)
- Go slow on important cuts
- Keep some extra wood

### Problem: Customer wants changes
**Solution:**
- Agree on design BEFORE starting
- Show sketch or photo
- Get deposit (materials cost)
- Extra changes = extra charge

## 🌳 Working With Wood

### Wood Types (Cheap to Expensive):
1. **Pine/Softwood** - Cheap, easy to work, not very strong
2. **Medium hardwood** - Good balance of cost and quality
3. **Premium hardwood** - Expensive, very strong, beautiful

### Wood Selection:
- **Furniture:** Medium to hard wood
- **Doors:** Hard wood (lasts longer)
- **Practice:** Soft wood or scrap
- **Outdoor:** Treated wood or hardwood

### Storing Wood:
- Keep dry (covered)
- Off the ground
- Flat (prevents warping)
- Good ventilation

## 🔒 Safety Important!

⚠️ **Always:**
- Keep tools sharp (dull = dangerous)
- Cut away from body
- Wear eye protection
- Check wood for nails before sawing
- Use stable work surface
- Keep work area clean
- Store tools safely

⚠️ **Never:**
- Rush important cuts
- Use broken tools
- Work when very tired
- Let children near power tools
- Force tools (if hard, tool wrong or dull)
