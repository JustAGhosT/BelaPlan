## 🥬 Leafy Greens (Quick Cash Crops)

### Spinach (30-45 days)

**Why Grow:**
- Fast turnover (6-8 harvests per year)
- High demand (everyone eats greens)
- Heat-tolerant varieties available
- Good for Bela Bela market

**Planting Guide:**
- **Season:** Year-round with irrigation, best Oct-March
- **Spacing:** 20cm between rows, 10cm in row
- **Seeds:** R15-25 per 100g packet
- **Plot size:** 10m x 10m = 500 plants
- **Water:** Daily in hot weather, every 2 days in cool

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Prepare bed - dig, add compost if available
2. Make shallow furrows (1cm deep)
3. Sprinkle seeds thinly
4. Cover lightly with soil
5. Water gently (don't wash away seeds)
6. Keep moist until germination (5-7 days)
7. Thin seedlings to 10cm apart
8. Water morning or evening (never midday in summer)
9. Harvest outer leaves first (plant continues producing)

**Bela Bela Tips:**
- Plant in partial shade during Dec-Feb (too hot)
- Use shade cloth if possible (30-50% shade)
- Mulch heavily to conserve water
- Morning watering best in summer heat

**Yield & Profit:**
- 10m x 10m plot: 30-50kg per harvest
- Harvest every 6 weeks
- Sell: R15-25 per kg
- Income: R450-1250 per harvest
- Cost: R50-80 (seeds, water)
- **Profit: R370-1170 per harvest**

### Amaranth/Morogo (25-40 days)

**Why Grow:**
- Even faster than spinach
- Traditional vegetable (high demand)
- Very heat-tolerant (perfect for Bela Bela)
- Drought-resistant

**Planting Guide:**
- **Season:** Year-round, thrives in heat
- **Spacing:** Broadcast or 15cm apart
- **Seeds:** R10-20 per packet
- **Plot size:** 10m x 10m
- **Water:** Less than spinach, drought-tolerant

**Growing:**
1. Broadcast seeds on prepared soil
2. Rake lightly to cover
3. Water once to settle
4. Germinates in 3-5 days
5. Thin if too dense
6. Can harvest leaves multiple times
7. Very easy to grow

**Market:**
- High demand in local market
- Tourist lodges buy for traditional meals
- Sell: R20-30 per kg
- Very profitable with low input

## 🍅 Fruiting Vegetables (High Value)

### Tomatoes (60-90 days)

**Why Grow:**
- High market demand
- Good prices year-round
- Multiple harvest from one plant
- Hotels/lodges need consistent supply

**Variety Selection for Bela Bela:**
- **Hot season (Oct-March):** Heat-tolerant varieties (Rodade, Money Maker)
- **Cool season (April-Sept):** Any variety works well
- **Cherry tomatoes:** Higher price, good for lodges

**Complete Growing Guide:**

**Seedling Stage (3-4 weeks):**
1. Start in seedling trays or small pots
2. Use good seedling mix (R30-50 per bag)
3. Plant seeds 1cm deep
4. Keep moist, not wet
5. Place in shade first week, then sun
6. Transplant when 10-15cm tall

**Field Preparation:**
1. Dig holes 50cm apart, 30cm deep
2. Add handful of compost or manure per hole
3. Mix well with soil
4. Water holes before planting

**Planting:**
1. Plant seedlings deep (bury stem up to first leaves)
2. Water immediately
3. Stake or tie to support
4. Mulch around plant

**Care:**
- **Water:** Daily in hot weather, deep watering better than frequent light
- **Feeding:** Liquid fertilizer every 2 weeks (or manure tea)
- **Pruning:** Remove suckers (side shoots) for bigger fruits
- **Staking:** Essential - tie as plant grows

**Pest Management (Bela Bela specific):**
- **Aphids:** Spray with soapy water
- **Cutworms:** Collar around seedling stem
- **Fruit worm:** Pick affected fruits quickly
- **Blight:** Avoid overhead watering, space plants well

**Harvest:**
- Pick when fully colored
- Harvest every 2-3 days in peak season
- Handle gently (bruises = no sale)
- Sell quickly (fresh = better price)

**Yield & Profit (20 plants):**
- Investment: R60-100
- Harvest: 80-150kg over 3 months
- Sell: R12-20 per kg
- Income: R960-3000
- **Profit: R860-2900**

**Bela Bela Market:**
- Hotels and lodges (regular orders)
- Roadside sales (good location on R101)
- Saturday market
- Direct to restaurants

### Peppers/Chilies (70-90 days)

**Why Grow:**
- Less common (less competition)
- Higher price than tomatoes
- Long harvest period (4-6 months)
- Heat-loving (perfect for Bela Bela)

**Types:**
- **Sweet peppers:** R30-50 per kg
- **Hot peppers:** R40-80 per kg
- **Both:** Steady demand

**Growing Similar to Tomatoes:**
- Start from seedlings
- Space 50cm apart
- Stake for support
- Water regularly
- Harvest when size and color right

**Advantage:**
- Less disease problems than tomatoes
- Longer production period
- Can dry chilies for storage
- Good tourist product (local hot sauce)

## 🥕 Root Vegetables

### Carrots (70-90 days)

**Why Grow:**
- Good storage (sell over time)
- Steady demand
- Less water than leafy greens
- Winter crop (April-August best)

**Growing Guide:**
1. Deep, loose soil essential (40cm deep)
2. Remove stones and clumps
3. Make furrows 2cm deep
4. Sow seeds thinly
5. Cover and water
6. Thin to 5cm spacing
7. Keep weeds down
8. Harvest when size right (test pull one)

**Bela Bela Season:**
- **Best:** April-August (cooler months)
- Avoid hot summer (November-February)
- Needs consistent moisture

---

### Beetroot (55-70 days)

**Why Grow:**
- Faster than carrots
- Easy to grow
- Both roots and leaves edible
- Heat-tolerant

**Simple Growing:**
1. Plant seeds 3cm apart
2. Cover lightly
3. Water regularly
4. Thin and eat thinnings as greens
5. Harvest when tennis ball size

**Market:**
- Sell fresh or pickled
- Lodges use for salads
- Good market demand

## 🥒 Other Profitable Vegetables

### Green Beans (50-60 days)

**Benefits:**
- Good price (R20-35 per kg)
- Easy to grow
- Multiple harvests
- Nitrogen fixer (improves soil)

**Types:**
- **Bush beans:** Easier, no support needed
- **Climbing beans:** Higher yield, need stakes

**Growing:**
- Plant 30cm spacing
- 3-4 seeds per hole
- Water regularly
- Harvest every 2-3 days when ready
- Keep picking to encourage more

---

### Cabbage (70-90 days)

**Why Grow:**
- Stores well
- Steady demand
- One harvest but big
- Good cool season crop

**Growing:**
- Start seedlings
- Transplant 50cm apart
- Needs consistent water
- Watch for pests (caterpillars)
- Harvest when firm and sized

## 💧 Irrigation Guide for Bela Bela

### Water Sources:

**Municipal Water:**
- Cost: R15-30 per 1000 liters
- Best for small plots
- Regular supply

**Borehole:**
- Initial cost: R8,000-15,000
- Free water after installation
- Best long-term investment
- Check water quality first

**Rainwater Harvesting:**
- Collect from roofs
- Store in tanks (expensive but worthwhile)
- Supplement other sources
- Use for dry season

### Irrigation Methods:

**Bucket/Watering Can:**
- **Good for:** Small plots (under 50m²)
- **Cost:** R30-80 (buckets/cans)
- **Labor:** High
- **Water efficiency:** Low

**Drip System:**
- **Good for:** Plots over 50m²
- **Cost:** R500-2000 for 100m²
- **Labor:** Low (automated)
- **Water efficiency:** High (save 50% water)
- **Best choice for Bela Bela heat**

**Sprinkler:**
- **Good for:** Leafy greens, large areas
- **Cost:** R200-1000
- **Water efficiency:** Medium
- **Watch for:** Evaporation in hot weather

### Water Schedule (Bela Bela):

**Hot Season (Oct-March):**
- Leafy greens: Daily, morning OR evening
- Tomatoes/peppers: Daily, deep watering
- Root vegetables: Every 2 days
- Time: Early morning (6-8am) or evening (5-7pm)
- **Never water midday** in summer!

**Cool Season (April-Sept):**
- Leafy greens: Every 2 days
- Tomatoes/peppers: Every 2-3 days
- Root vegetables: Every 3-4 days
- Can water any time (less evaporation)

## 🌾 Crop Rotation Plan

### Why Rotate:
- Prevents soil exhaustion
- Reduces pests and diseases
- Improves soil fertility
- Better yields

### Simple 3-Year Rotation:

**Year 1:**
- Plot A: Leafy greens
- Plot B: Tomatoes/peppers
- Plot C: Beans

**Year 2:**
- Plot A: Tomatoes/peppers
- Plot B: Beans
- Plot C: Leafy greens

**Year 3:**
- Plot A: Beans
- Plot B: Leafy greens
- Plot C: Tomatoes/peppers

### Rules:
- Don't plant same family in same spot
- Beans improve soil (plant before heavy feeders)
- Rotate yearly minimum

## 🛒 Marketing Your Vegetables in Bela Bela

### Direct Sales:

**Saturday Market:**
- Best day for sales
- Arrive early (6am) for good spot
- Display attractively
- Price competitively

**Roadside Stand:**
- Need visible location
- Fresh stock daily
- Shade for produce
- Municipal permission

**Door-to-Door:**
- Start with neighbors
- Build regular customer base
- Weekly delivery
- Fresh guarantee

### Bulk Sales:

**Hotels and Lodges:**
- Regular orders (weekly/bi-weekly)
- Need consistent supply
- Slightly lower price but guaranteed sales
- Deliver early morning

**Restaurants:**
- Chef wants quality and consistency
- Establish relationship
- Agree on varieties they want
- Weekly supply

**Shops:**
- Spaza shops and superettes
- Regular supply essential
- Price competitive
- Fresh and quality important

### Pricing Strategy:

**Check Market:**
- Visit Bela Bela market weekly
- Know current prices
- Price at or slightly below

**Quality Premium:**
- Extra fresh = higher price
- Organic/pesticide-free = 20-30% more
- Consistent size and quality = better price

**Volume Discount:**
- Hotels buying bulk = 10-20% discount
- Still profitable with steady sales

## 📊 Profitable Crop Calendar (Bela Bela)

### Rainy Season (Nov-March):

**Plant:**
- Tomatoes (Nov for Dec-Feb harvest)
- Peppers
- Amaranth
- Beans
- Pumpkins

**Benefits:**
- Free water from rain
- Fast growth
- High yields

**Challenges:**
- Disease pressure
- Hail risk (Dec-Jan)
- Competition (everyone planting)

### Dry Season (April-Oct):

**Plant (with irrigation):**
- Spinach
- Carrots
- Beetroot
- Cabbage
- Onions

**Benefits:**
- Higher prices (less competition)
- Less disease
- Better quality
- Steady production

**Challenges:**
- Water cost/availability
- More irrigation labor
